The Library of Babel

An endless library you can walk. It is made of hexagonal galleries — each with four walls of bookshelves (700 books per gallery) and two hallways that open onto the next gallery, plus a staircase up and down.

Nothing here is stored. Every gallery and every book is generated on the spot from its coordinates — so the same place always holds exactly the same books, forever, for everyone. The Library is infinite, yet it weighs almost nothing: only your path is remembered.

How to wander

  • ◁ ▷ hallways · ▲ ▼ stairs — or use the arrow keys.
  • Click the gallery (z, n) coordinate to jump anywhere — leap as far across the lattice as you like.
  • The minimap (left) shows the four exits and their hashes — click one to move.
  • The alphabet menu switches you into a different library entirely: new text, new hashes.
  • copy link (or click the header hash) copies a permalink straight back to this spot.

Reading a book

  • Click any colored spine to open it; arrow keys turn its 410 pages.
  • color redraws the page as a character-color map — each symbol a square of colour, tinted by the gallery's hash.
  • save… → borrow book takes the full text home (~1.3 MB .txt); book image renders the whole book as one picture you can save png.

The numbers in the header

gallery (z, n) is your coordinate. hash is a fingerprint of this gallery's 700 books — a permalink and proof, identical every time you return. Your last 50 galleries are kept in full; every step's hash is kept forever. export saves your whole journey; new walk drops you somewhere random.

Inspiration

Jorge Luis Borges, “The Library of Babel” (1941).
